Spin Glass Theory

An Introduction to Statistical Physics of Disordered Systems

This course introduces the theory of spin glasses, a fascinating field at the intersection of statistical physics, mathematics, and computer science. From physical systems with random interactions to modern applications in machine learning and optimization, spin glass theory provides essential tools and insights for understanding complex systems.

  • Lecture 1: Background, Motivation, and REM
    Introduction to spin glasses, physical motivation, and the Random Energy Model as a simple example.
  • Lecture 2: The Sherrington-Kirkpatrick Model
    Introduction to the SK model, mean-field theory, and the emergence of replica symmetry breaking.
  • Lecture 3: The Replica Method – Basics and REM
    Introduction to the replica trick and its application to the Random Energy Model.
  • Lecture 4: Replica Method for the SK Model
    Application of replica method to SK model, replica symmetry breaking, and Parisi's solution.
  • Lecture 5: Rigorous Methods
    Gaussian comparison inequalities, Guerra's interpolation method, and rigorous results.
  • Lecture 6: The Cavity Method
    Introduction to cavity method and its application to simple models.
  • Lecture 7: TAP Equations
    Thouless-Anderson-Palmer equations and their physical interpretation.
  • Lecture 8: Applications and AMP
    Modern applications including compressed sensing and approximate message passing.
  • Supplementary Material
    Additional topics, open problems, and current research directions.
