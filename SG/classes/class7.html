Lecture 7: TAP Equations and the SK Model

The Cavity Method Applied to SK Model
Lecture Notes

Overview: We now apply the cavity method to the SK model, deriving the Thouless-Anderson-Palmer (TAP) equations. These self-consistency equations for local magnetizations include the crucial Onsager reaction term, absent in naive mean-field theory. We discuss their derivation, physical meaning, and connection to both replica and dynamics approaches.

Cavity Method for SK Model

Recall the SK Hamiltonian:

$$ H_N(\sigma) = -\frac{1}{\sqrt{N}}\sum_{i

Fix spin $i$ and remove it (make a cavity). The effective field on $i$ from the remaining spins is:

$$ h_i = \frac{1}{\sqrt{N}}\sum_{j(\neq i)} J_{ij}\langle \sigma_j \rangle_{(i)} $$

where $\langle \cdot \rangle_{(i)}$ denotes thermal average in the cavity system (without spin $i$).

The Onsager Reaction Term

A crucial insight: When we put spin $i$ back, it affects the other spins, which then react back on $i$. This leads to:

  • Direct term: $h_i = \frac{1}{\sqrt{N}}\sum_{j(\neq i)} J_{ij}m_j$
  • Reaction (Onsager) term: $-\beta(1-q)m_i$ where $q = \frac{1}{N}\sum_j m_j^2$

The full TAP equations are:

$$ m_i = \tanh\Big(\beta\big[\frac{1}{\sqrt{N}}\sum_{j(\neq i)} J_{ij}m_j - \beta(1-q)m_i\big]\Big) $$

for each spin $i$, where $m_i = \langle \sigma_i \rangle$ is the local magnetization.

Derivation of the Onsager Term

The reaction term comes from a careful cavity calculation:

  1. When spin $i$ is present with magnetization $m_i$, it creates a field $J_{ij}m_i/\sqrt{N}$ on spin $j$
  2. This changes $m_j$ by $\beta J_{ij}m_i/\sqrt{N}$ (linear response)
  3. The modified $m_j$ values create a reaction field on $i$
  4. Summing over paths $i\to j\to i$ gives the $\beta(1-q)m_i$ term

Properties of TAP Equations

  • High temperature: Only solution is $m_i=0$ for all $i$ (paramagnetic)
  • Below $T_c=1$: Many solutions appear, corresponding to different pure states
  • Free energy: Each solution is a stationary point of the TAP free energy functional:
$$ F_{TAP}(\{m_i\}) = -\frac{1}{\sqrt{N}}\sum_{i

where $S(m) = -\frac{1+m}{2}\ln\frac{1+m}{2} - \frac{1-m}{2}\ln\frac{1-m}{2}$ is the single-spin entropy.

Connection to Replica Results

The TAP approach gives complementary insight to replica theory:

  • RS solution: corresponds to finding a single TAP solution
  • RSB: reflects existence of many TAP solutions with complex structure
  • Both approaches predict same thermodynamics when properly interpreted

Numerical Solution and Dynamics

The TAP equations can be solved iteratively:

  1. Start with random initial $\{m_i\}$
  2. Update each $m_i$ using the equation until convergence
  3. Different initial conditions find different solutions

This suggests a connection to dynamics:

  • Each TAP solution is a possible equilibrium state
  • Real dynamics gets trapped near one solution
  • Aging phenomena reflect slow evolution between solutions

Beyond SK: Message Passing

TAP equations have inspired powerful algorithms:

  • Belief Propagation: Generalizes cavity method to arbitrary graphs
  • Survey Propagation: Handles multiple pure states (RSB)
  • Applications: Error correction, satisfiability, compressed sensing

Key ideas that transfer:

  • Local fields/messages capture essential physics
  • Must include reaction effects (like Onsager term)
  • Multiple solutions indicate complex energy landscape

TAP Equations and Complexity

The number of TAP solutions $\mathcal{N}(f)$ at free energy density $f$ defines a complexity $\Sigma(f)$:

$$ \Sigma(f) = \lim_{N\to\infty} \frac{1}{N}\ln \mathcal{N}(f) $$

Properties:

  • $\Sigma(f)$ is non-negative only in a range $[f_{\min}, f_{\max}]$
  • Physical states concentrate near $f_{\min}$ (dominated by complexity)
  • This picture matches Parisi's RSB solution

Summary (Lecture 7): The TAP equations provide a physical picture of the SK model's behavior through local magnetizations and reaction fields. They reveal the emergence of many solutions below $T_c$, connecting to replica theory's prediction of many pure states. The cavity/TAP perspective has inspired practical algorithms and remains a key tool in spin glass theory.
